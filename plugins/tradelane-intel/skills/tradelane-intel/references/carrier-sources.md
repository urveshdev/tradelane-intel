# Where carriers publish their networks (public sources) — with direct links

Fetch order — most reliable / most citable first:

1. **Alliance product documents (PDFs).** Full east–west network booklets with rotations. Open,
   stable, the single best citable source for mainline services.
2. **Trade-press service-change posts.** For *dated* launches/withdrawals and specific rotations.
   Best for "is this new / when did it change".
3. **Carrier own service pages / finders.** Most complete but often JS-rendered or bot-protected —
   use when reachable; otherwise reconstruct from (1)+(2). See `fetching.md` for recovery.

## Ports & throughput (for hub / transshipment questions)

- **Port-authority statistics** — the port's own site (most citable for volume). Examples:
  Tanger Med https://www.tangermed.ma/en/ · Colombo/SLPA https://www.slpa.lk/ ·
  Cai Mep–Thi Vai / Vietnam Maritime Admin https://vpa.org.vn/ ·
  Manzanillo (API) https://www.puertomanzanillo.com.mx/ · Vizhinjam https://vizhinjamport.in/
- **Trade press port coverage** — WorldCargo News, Container News, Maritime Gateway, JOC, Lloyd's List (above).
- **Transshipment vs gateway** — check the port's transshipment *share* and hinterland size; a hub
  that "cannibalises" another transshipment terminal's volume is doing relay, not local gateway.

## Reliability note

Alliance PDFs and port-authority stats are the gold standard for citation. Trade-press posts are
excellent for *dates* and *specifics* — confirm the outlet is one of the reputable names above (avoid
SEO/aggregator re-posts). When two sources disagree on a rotation, prefer the carrier/alliance's own
document and say so.
