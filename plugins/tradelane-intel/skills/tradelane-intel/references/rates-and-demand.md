# Freight-rate & demand sources (the market layer) — with direct links

Use these when the question touches rates, capacity, or demand. Fetch the URL directly; only fall
back to WebSearch if a page has moved. All are third-party (not carrier-published) — cite them as
market commentary, distinct from carrier-published network facts.

## Spot-rate direction (which way, how fast)

- **Drewry — World Container Index (WCI)** — weekly, 8 major lanes; the standard reference (free
  weekly headline). https://www.drewry.co.uk/supply-chain-advisors/supply-chain-expertise/world-container-index-assessed-by-drewry
- **Freightos — Baltic Index (FBX)** https://terminal.freightos.com/
  and the plain-language weekly updates ("what moved and why") https://www.freightos.com/freight-industry-updates/
- **Shanghai Shipping Exchange — SCFI / CCFI** (SCFI has a Shanghai–Australia/NZ line):
  SCFI https://en.sse.net.cn/indices/scfinew.jsp · CCFI https://en.sse.net.cn/indices/ccfinew.jsp
- **Xeneta** — spot-vs-long-term commentary/press https://www.xeneta.com/resources
- **Carrier market updates** — carrier-published, lane-level rate + capacity notes (citable):
  Maersk https://www.maersk.com/news/

For a *specific* corridor with no index line (e.g. China→Australia FCL), use forwarder market
advisories — label as forwarder estimates and corroborate *direction* against an index first:
Seabridge (AU) https://seabridge.com.au/newsroom/market-update/ ·
Bertling https://www.bertling.com/news-pool/ · C.H. Robinson https://www.chrobinson.com/en-us/resources/

## Capacity & discipline (the supply side)

- **Sea-Intelligence** — blank-sailing & schedule-reliability reports https://www.sea-intelligence.com/press-room
- **Drewry** — blank-sailing / capacity trackers (same domain as WCI above).
- **Alphaliner** — fleet, orderbook, carrier market share https://www.alphaliner.com/ ·
  **Linerlytica** — capacity & market commentary https://www.linerlytica.com/

## Demand & volumes (the pull side — critical for the divergence test)

- **Container Trades Statistics (CTS)** — actual TEU by lane https://www.containerstatistics.com/
- **Descartes Datamyne** — import-volume trackers (US & others) https://www.descartes.com/resources
- **National trade data** — customs/statistics agencies, e.g. Australia ABS
  https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/economy/international-trade ; US Census/Descartes.
- **Port throughput** — the port authority's own monthly (see `carrier-sources.md` for port links).

## The one test that beats "peak season"

A rate rise *with* rising demand is a demand rally (often seasonal). A rate rise *while demand is
flat or falling* is **supply discipline** — carriers withholding space. Always pull the demand line
next to the rate line before attributing a move; the divergence is where the non-obvious story lives.
